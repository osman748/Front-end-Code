Classic French Toast Recipe

Bienvenue, Oh la la! There is nothing quite like the aroma of golden, buttery French Toast to pull you out of bed on a sleepy morning. By taking simple, everyday bread and soaking it in a silky custard of eggs and milk, you transform basic ingredients into a delicious, melt-in-your-mouth masterpiece. Whether you pile it high with fresh berries or let a lake of maple syrup soak into every corner, this classic recipe is the ultimate comfort food for any breakfast table.

Ingredients

  • Bread: 8 thick slices of bread (Brioche, Challah, or Texas Toast are highly recommended) [2, 11].Eggs: 4 large eggs [2, 12, 33].
  • Milk: 2/3 cup whole milk (or half-and-half for a richer flavor) [12, 24].
  • Flavourings: 1 tsp vanilla extract, 1 tsp ground cinnamon, and a pinch of salt [2, 33].
  • Sweetener: 1 tbsp granulated sugar (optional) [2, 28].
  • Cooking: 2 tbsp unsalted butter [2, 35].

Instructions

  1. Whisk the Custard: In a shallow dish or wide bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla, cinnamon, sugar, and salt until completely smooth [2, 17, 33].
  2. Dip the Bread: Briefly dip each slice of bread into the custard. Soak for about 10–15 seconds per side so it's saturated but not falling apart [2, 35].
  3. Heat the Pan: Melt 1 tablespoon of butter in a large non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat [2, 33].
  4. Fry until Golden: Place the soaked bread in the skillet and cook for 2–3 minutes per side until golden brown and slightly crisp [2, 28].
  5. Serve: Serve immediately with your favorite toppings, such as maple syrup, fresh berries, or a dusting of powdered sugar [2, 12, 24].
